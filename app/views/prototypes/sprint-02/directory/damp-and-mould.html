Damp and mould

If you have a problem with damp and mould, you must tell your landlord and ideally put your concerns in writing.

Keep a record of all conversations, emails and messages.

Government guidance says that landlords should take damp and mould seriously.

Your landlord must try to find out what's causing the problem.

You should give your landlord access to your home to check the issue.

They can contact you to agree a suitable date and time to visit.

If your landlord cannot identify the cause of the problem, they should ask a damp surveyor. You do not have to pay for this.

Preventing damp and mould where possible

It’s important to ventilate your property, especially after activities like cooking or showering. If you can keep a consistent, low-level background heat, this can also help prevent condensation.

For more details and advice, visit the Shelter website’s section on damp and mould.
